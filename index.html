Key Insight for UXR
s_cube_id, s_till_id, and s_risk_zone appear in every in-session event. Every kill, death, loot, and combat action can be sliced by Cube face, tile, and risk zone — the data infrastructure for Cube Rotation analysis already exists.

A Session's Data Flow

Matchmaking
Player initiates matchmaking with loadout, party, ELO
matchmakingStart → matchmakingEnd (result + duration + fail reason)
Deploy
Full "entry snapshot": loadout (every weapon + attachment + gear with unique_id), backpack, accepted quests, game settings, skins
playerDeploy — the richest single event in the system
Parachute
Start position → landing position + flight distance + duration
playerParachuteStart → playerParachuteDown
In-Session Loop — 10s heartbeat + event-driven
playerPosition every 10s: location, orientation, backpack value, team distance, quest progress, FPS percentiles (25p/50p/75p/95p/99p), Ping percentiles, packet loss, player state

playerReceivedDamage every 10s: damage breakdown, source (PvE/Boss/Player), FPS/Ping snapshot, both sides' full gear + stats

cubeRotation: rotation reason, before/after tile layout, risk zone changes, affected player count

Combat: skillUse, isDown, death (full killer info + loss value), revived, heal, shieldRestore
Loot: combatItemAdded/Removed (item_unique_id tracking + source attribution)
Boss/PvE: bossEventStats, PveEnermyBattleStats (per-player DPS/heal/skill breakdown)
World: worldEventOccurred (type, severity 1-5, trigger reason incl. cube_rotation)
Exit / Settlement
playerLeaveServer: leave reason, duration, distance, backpack value, loadout, risk zone duration JSON (seconds per W/Y/P), POI count, Cube face count, Tile count, kills/deaths/encounters per risk zone, completed quests, extraction point ID, loss breakdown
Part II

Session Quality Framework

Derived from The Cube's core design intent.

The Logic: Core Promise → Session Quality

A good SQ framework answers: "This game promises X. Did this session deliver X?"

Typical Extraction Shooter
"Survive and loot out."
One-dimensional tension → SQ = extraction rate + loot ROI
vs.
The Cube's Promise
"In a world that keeps changing, choose your own risk, leave with purpose, and make your persistent world better."

This promise decomposes into three core pursuits that define session quality:

Risk Zone Rules

The three-tier risk system is the backbone of player agency in The Cube.

White — Top Face
  • No PvP allowed
  • PvE only, lower-tier enemies
  • Lowest loot value
  • Safe learning ground
Yellow — Four Sides
  • PvP allowed
  • Respawn available on death
  • Partial gear loss on death
  • Balanced risk-reward
Purple — Bottom Face
  • PvP — full exposure
  • No respawn
  • Full gear loss on death
  • Highest loot + top-tier Bosses
Purple Zone Paradox
Design claims "risk is optional." But top-tier Bosses only spawn in Purple → progression-driven players are functionally forced into the highest-risk zone. Whether this feels like agency or coercion is a P0 validation question.
1
Dynamic Stakes
Is this session's world alive and different from the last one?
"Every session should feel like a new situation, not a repeated route."
A — Cube Rotation Experience
A1. Rotation Exposure
% of sessions where player experienced ≥1 rotation
cubeRotation per deployment_id
A2. Behavioral Impact
Movement change within 60s post-rotation vs. 60s pre-rotation
playerPosition diff around cubeRotation
A3. Risk Reset Rate
% of players whose risk_zone changed due to rotation (not movement)
cubeRotation.risk_zone_changes
B — Emergent Encounter Diversity
B1. Encounter Diversity
Distinct encounter types per session (PvE / Boss / PvP / World Event / Arena)
battle + death + worldEvent + arena
B2. PvP Density by Zone
Player encounters per minute in each risk zone
playerLeaveServer.s_player_encounter
B3. World Event Participation
% of sessions overlapping a world event × behavioral change during
worldEventOccurred

Only UXR Can Answer

  • Did the player notice the rotation? Understand what changed? Did it change their plan?
  • Did encounters feel "emergent and exciting" or "random and unfair"?
  • After rotation moved them from Yellow to Purple — opportunity or punishment?
2
Risk-Choice Agency
Is the player choosing risk, or being chosen by risk?
"I decide how much danger I want. Higher risk is more rewarding, but never forced."
C — Risk Selection Behavior
C1. Zone Time Split
% of session time in White : Yellow : Purple
playerLeaveServer.s_risk_zone_duration
C2. Risk-Reward Gradient
Loot value per minute, by risk zone
combatItemAdded.i_item_value × risk_zone
C3. Voluntary vs. Forced
% of zone upgrades by player movement vs. rotation pushing them
playerPosition vs. cubeRotation.risk_zone_changes
C4. Purple Penetration × Survival
% sessions entering purple × extraction rate from purple
risk_zone=purple + leave_reason
D — Death Cost & Gear Fear
D1. Death Loss Distribution
Percentile distribution of total_loss_value on death
playerDeath.f_total_loss_value
D2. Post-Death Behavior
After death: re-queue vs. Vine vs. logout — and time to next action
playerDeath → next matchmakingStart
D3. Gear Fear Index
Trend of loadout_value across consecutive deploys per player
playerDeploy.i_loadout_value sequence

Only UXR Can Answer

  • "Did you want to go to Purple, or feel you had to?"
  • "When you died, was it your mistake or unfair?"
  • "After losing gear, did you want to try again or stop playing?"
  • Distinguish risk aversion by choice (healthy) vs. by confusion (design problem)
3
Purposeful Extraction
Is the player leaving with intent, or fleeing in panic?
"The key moment is not just WHEN to leave — but WHY you choose to leave." — R&D

Unlike typical extraction shooters where SQ ≈ extraction rate + loot ROI, The Cube adds a purpose layer: players should extract not just to preserve loot but to advance their persistent world. A panic extraction with trash loot is not higher quality than a death after completing a high-value contract — it depends on whether the session had purpose.

E — Extraction Quality
E1. Extraction Rate
extraction / total sessions (baseline)
playerLeaveServer.leave_reason
E2. Loot ROI
(exit backpack_value − entry backpack_value) / entry loadout_value
playerLeaveServer − playerDeploy
E3. Intentional Exit Rate
% exits that are extraction (proactive) vs. death/disconnect/quit (reactive)
playerLeaveServer.leave_reason
E4. Contract-Driven Rate
% sessions with ≥1 accepted contract at entry
playerDeploy.s_mission_status
E5. Contract Completion
Of contract-driven sessions, % that completed ≥1 contract
playerLeaveServer.s_mission_finished
F — Session Rhythm
F1. Duration Distribution
Percentile distribution of session length (find natural rhythm)
playerLeaveServer.i_duration
F2. Exploration Breadth
Tiles × POIs × Cube faces visited
playerLeaveServer: till/poi/cube_num
F3. Re-Queue Momentum
Time to next matchmaking by exit type (extraction vs. death vs. quit)
playerLeaveServer → next matchmakingStart

Only UXR Can Answer

  • "Why did you extract at that moment?" (loot full? contract done? scared? bored?)
  • "Did you go in with a plan? Did you accomplish it?"
  • "After extracting, did you know what to do with your loot?" (purpose clarity)
  • "Would you call this session successful? Why?" (subjective vs. data quality)

The Data–UXR Bridge

What telemetry can see vs. what only user research can reveal.

DATA Can Measure

  • What happened — actions, sequences, frequencies
  • How many — distributions, rates, proportions
  • When & where — timestamps, coordinates, zones
  • Correlations — FPS×death, zone×loot, loadout×survival

UXR Must Answer

  • Why — motivation, decision rationale
  • How it felt — tension, fairness, satisfaction
  • Understanding — did they get the mechanic?
  • Intent — voluntary vs. forced, planned vs. reactive
The 5 Critical Gaps for Alpha
Cube Rotation ComprehensionData sees behavioral change. Only UXR distinguishes perceived → understood → influenced decision.
Purple Zone AgencyData sees who enters purple. Only UXR tells "I chose to" from "I had to" for progression.
Extraction MotivationSame extraction rate, different meaning: "I got what I came for" vs. "I'm about to die."
PvP Encounter IntentSame event. "I wanted that fight" vs. "I was ambushed" produce opposite experiences.
Death AttributionSame death. "My mistake, I'll learn" retains. "Unfair, the game cheated" churns.
